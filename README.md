# homelessness-housing-affordability-final-project
Outline:

This project is about the relationship between affordable housing availability and homelessness in DC from 2020 to 2025. In 2024, there was a shift in this relationship, showing affordable housing increasing as homelessness also increased. This story acts as a way to explore why this shift occurred and the factors that are not including in these two variables as they are in data. 
Data:

I compiled this dataset from Open Data DC and the PIT Report from the last 5 years. The data from Open Data DC shows the affordable housing availability/construction since as far back as 2015 and projects housing that will be done in the future. For my purposes, I consolidated this data into just 2020-2025 to have a more concise and relevant dataset. The PIT Report is collected every year by DC as well, and I took the homelessness numbers from each report from 2020-2025. 
Analysis:

I compared each year's homelessness numbers with the number of avaiable affordable housing and new affordable housing. I calculated how many units were available and built in each year and compared the trend of these numbers from 2020-2025 with the homelessness pattern from the same years. 
Limitations:

One major limitation in this data is that neither report accounts for population shifts, which could impact how many homeless people there are or how much housing is available. Additionally, it does not show who qualifies for affordable housing, if people are being evicted or the limitations construction companies are facing. There could be less land to build on, as well as restrictions on who can access the housing. These variables are crucial to understanding why this shift has taken place, and therefore needs to be explored to better understand the patterns the data presents.
